About Tri Vananda

A place to live,
not to escape to.

Tri Vananda is a 232-acre well-living community in the quiet northeast of Phuket. A place where body, mind, and family life improve together — not on a schedule, but as a way of living.

Nine lakes. A regenerative farm. A forest left mostly as it was. Private pool villas, fully furnished, ready to live in. Everything your family needs, inside one ecosystem.

The Feeling

A community built
around the lives
you love most.

You have already built the business. The career. The home. What you come here for is the other part of the life — the one you always said you would get around to. Here, you finally do.

Mornings in the forest. Long lunches with the family. Neighbours who become friends across generations. Owning a residence at Tri Vananda is not about a second address. It is about the quiet feeling of having chosen well — for yourself, and for everyone you love.

The Place

A landscape, first.
A place, built gently.
The rest, left to nature.

At the centre of the community is the Community House — a swimming pool, an Active Movement Studio, a Tweenie Club for the younger ones, and Jampa, a Michelin Green Star restaurant. Health Resort by Clinique La Prairie at Tri Vananda — the 90-year Swiss health institution's first resort in Southeast Asia — sits inside the community. Nine lakes, a wetland reserve, and an abundance of nature hold the rest. Fifteen minutes from the airport. Fifteen minutes from the beach. Everything your family needs, nothing you don't.

The Residences

Two styles.
Seven biophilic layouts.
Fully furnished.

The private pool villas at Tri Vananda come in two styles — Vana and Nanda — and seven floor plans, from 325 to 780 square metres. Two to four bedrooms. Each villa has a private pool, en-suite bathrooms, and ceilings up to 6.7 metres. Fully furnished and equipped, ready to live in from the day you arrive.

Collection I

Vana

Timber, courtyards, and canopy. A biophilic design rooted in natural materials, where the rooms open out into the forest and the forest quietly steps back in.
